Wet Waste & Composting Hub

Managed by the Goregaon Green Warriors Group — turning kitchen waste into nutrient-rich soil.

Why Compost Your Kitchen Waste?

Over 60% of Mumbai's household garbage is wet organic waste. Composting at home drastically reduces landfill strain, eliminates toxic methane emissions, and creates free fertiliser for your plants and gardens.

♻️ Reduces landfill loadOrganic waste in landfills produces methane — 25× worse than CO₂ for climate impact.
🌿 Free fertiliserFinished compost replaces chemical fertilisers and improves soil quality.
💧 Saves waterCompost-enriched soil holds moisture 3× better, reducing watering needs.
🐛 No smell, no pestsA well-managed compost bin has no odour and doesn't attract rodents.

The Mumbai Apartment Composting Guide

Living in a Mumbai high-rise doesn't mean you can't compost! Here is a foolproof, odor-free method designed specifically for small balconies and compact spaces, aligned with BMC's on-site waste processing mandate.

✅ The "Greens" (Wet)

  • Vegetable peels & fruit skins
  • Tea leaves & coffee grounds
  • Crushed eggshells
  • Leftover cooked food (rinse off oil)

🍂 The "Browns" (Dry)

  • Dry leaves (from local parks)
  • Cocopeat (local nurseries)
  • Torn brown cardboard
  • Shredded non-glossy newspaper
1
Choose Your Bin: Get a breathable terracotta Khamba (layered pots) or a repurposed plastic bucket. Crucial: Drill multiple holes around the sides and bottom. Mumbai's humidity requires maximum airflow to prevent odors.
2
Create the Base Layer: Start with a 2-inch layer of dry "Browns" at the bottom of your bin. This absorbs excess moisture from the wet waste—a common issue during Mumbai's heavy monsoons.
3
The 1:2 Ratio Rule: For every bowl of wet kitchen "Greens" you add, top it off with two bowls of dry "Browns". Mix them well or layer them. The browns act as a biofilter, completely masking any smells.
4
Aerate & Maintain: Once a week, use a stick or trowel to turn the mixture, giving it oxygen. If it gets too soggy or smells sour, add more cocopeat or dry leaves. If it's too dry and dusty, sprinkle a few drops of buttermilk or water.
5
Harvest Your 'Black Gold': In 45 to 60 days, your waste will transform into a dark, crumbly, earthy-smelling compost. Use it to supercharge your balcony plants or donate it to your housing society's garden!
🚫 What NOT to compost: Meat, bones, dairy products, glossy magazines, heavily oily/spicy curries, or plastic of any kind. These will attract pests and ruin the pile.
